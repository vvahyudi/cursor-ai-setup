# Methodology Notes

## Topic selection

I selected **LinkedIn organic content strategy for B2B SaaS** because it has strong practitioner coverage across LinkedIn posts, YouTube videos, podcasts, newsletters, and owned websites. The topic is also timely: LinkedIn is investing in creator, video, events, and B2B creator-marketplace products, making organic authority and practitioner-led content increasingly important for B2B SaaS teams.

## Inclusion criteria for experts

Each expert should meet at least two of the following:

1. Actively publishes on LinkedIn, YouTube, podcast, or owned newsletter.
2. Has direct operating experience in B2B SaaS/content/demand/community/creator-led growth.
3. Teaches frameworks grounded in their own work, client work, or company/community building.
4. Has source material that can be collected reproducibly: profile, post URLs, podcast episode pages, YouTube video IDs, or official site.

## Exclusion criteria

- Generic marketing bloggers with no evidence of direct execution.
- Influencer-only accounts where the topic is mainly motivational/personal branding without B2B SaaS relevance.
- Sources that only summarize others without primary examples.

## Source hierarchy

1. Practitioner-owned site / official profile.
2. LinkedIn post or article from the practitioner.
3. YouTube/podcast episode featuring the practitioner.
4. Company/community profile confirming role and body of work.
5. Third-party interview only when it adds concrete claims or history not present elsewhere.

## Reproducibility protocol

- Save raw URL, title, author, publication date, capture date, and collection method.
- Store raw content separately from analyst notes.
- Record whether full text came from an API, manual capture, transcript API, or public-index snippet.
- Never treat search-result snippets as complete post text.
