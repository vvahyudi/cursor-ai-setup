# Synthesis Seeds

These are preliminary research themes to validate after full post/transcript capture.

## 1. Founder-led content beats company-page-only content early

Tommy Clark, Sam Dunning, and Daniel Murray all point toward putting founders/executives/practitioners forward instead of hiding behind a logo. This should become a playbook section on founder selection, voice capture, ghostwriting workflow, and cadence.

## 2. Zero-click value increases trust and native reach

Amanda Natividad's zero-click marketing framework suggests that high-value native posts can build trust without forcing clicks. LinkedIn organic playbooks should include standalone educational posts, mini-frameworks, teardown posts, and POVs that are complete in-feed.

## 3. Distribution is a system, not a last step

Ross Simmonds' create-once/distribute-forever philosophy supports a workflow where one insight becomes a LinkedIn post, carousel, video, newsletter, podcast segment, sales enablement asset, and short-form clip.

## 4. LinkedIn should influence pipeline, not just create last-click conversions

Chris Walker, Sam Dunning, and George Terry sources imply that LinkedIn often works through dark social, awareness, sales-pipeline influence, and warm ICP exposure rather than clean last-click lead gen.

## 5. Creator-led / relationship-led GTM is replacing faceless distribution

Nick Bennett and Daniel Murray are useful for the section on B2B influence, category experts, employees as creators, and creator-brand partnerships.

## 6. Strategy starts with what you want to be known for

Devin Reed's content-system positioning supports a knowledge-base module around positioning, executive POV, content pillars, and trust-before-demand.
